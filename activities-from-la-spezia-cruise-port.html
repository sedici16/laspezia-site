Getting from the Port to Your Activity

La Spezia's cruise terminal sits right in the city center. The main train station â€” La Spezia Centrale â€” is about a 15-minute walk from the port, or a quick taxi ride. From that station, you can reach virtually every destination covered in this guide. Trains run frequently and the network is reliable. For more remote experiences, such as private excursions into the hills, transfers are typically arranged directly by your tour operator.

One important note for cruise passengers: always build in a buffer before your ship departs. Italian trains are generally punctual, but delays happen. If you're planning independent travel to destinations like Cinque Terre or Lucca, aim to be back at the port at least 90 minutes before all-aboard time.

The Cinque Terre: Liguria's Most Famous Attraction

The five villages of Cinque Terre are the default day trip for La Spezia cruise passengers â€” and for good reason. Perched on dramatic cliff faces above the Ligurian Sea, these colorful fishing villages are unlike anything else in Italy. Trains from La Spezia reach the first village, Riomaggiore, in under 10 minutes.

If you're considering which village to visit, each has a distinct character. Monterosso al Mare is the largest, with a proper beach. Vernazza is widely regarded as the most picturesque. Manarola is ideal for photography and views. Riomaggiore is the closest and easiest to reach. Corniglia sits atop a cliff and requires a climb of nearly 400 steps from the train station.

For a deeper breakdown, read our guide to visiting Cinque Terre from La Spezia cruise port, which covers transport, timing, and how to avoid the worst of the crowds.

Porto Venere: The Underrated Alternative

Most cruise passengers head straight to Cinque Terre and miss Porto Venere entirely. That's a mistake worth avoiding. This walled medieval town sits at the tip of the peninsula that shelters the Gulf of La Spezia, and it's genuinely stunning â€” a UNESCO World Heritage Site with dramatically different character from its more famous neighbors.

The walk along the sea walls, the Church of San Pietro perched above the cliffs, the ruins of the Doria Castle â€” Porto Venere rewards slow exploration. Boats run regularly from La Spezia's city waterfront. The journey takes about 25 minutes and the views coming into the harbor are remarkable.

Lerici: A Coastal Village for Those Who Prefer Quiet

Lerici is a small, genuinely beautiful coastal town on the eastern shore of the Gulf of La Spezia. It takes about 10 minutes by taxi or 30 minutes by local bus from the cruise port. Unlike Cinque Terre, Lerici rarely sees large tour groups. You get the same Ligurian atmosphere â€” colorful buildings, seafood restaurants, a medieval castle overlooking the bay â€” without the crowds.

Percy Bysshe Shelley lived here in the last months of his life, and the town maintains a cultured, unhurried pace that feels genuinely Italian rather than tourist-oriented. The beaches here are among the most accessible near the port.

Beaches Near La Spezia

The coastline around the Gulf of La Spezia includes several excellent swimming beaches. Lerici and nearby Fiascherino have clear water and a relaxed atmosphere. Monterosso al Mare in Cinque Terre has the region's best sandy beach. For cruise passengers who want to spend their port day swimming and relaxing rather than sightseeing, the beaches here are genuinely worth it â€” especially in summer.
