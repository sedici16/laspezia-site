Lerici from La Spezia Cruise Port: The Charming Village Most Passengers Overlook

On the eastern shore of the Gulf of La Spezia, directly across the water from Porto Venere, sits Lerici â€” a small, genuinely beautiful coastal town that most cruise passengers walk straight past on their way to the train station. This is their loss. Lerici is everything that Cinque Terre has become too famous to be: relaxed, authentic, relatively uncrowded, and possessed of a quality of light and setting that made it a favorite of English Romantic poets for a reason.

Percy Bysshe Shelley spent the last months of his life here, in the Casa Magni on the waterfront, before drowning in the gulf in 1822. D.H. Lawrence lived in nearby Fiascherino. Virginia Woolf wrote about the quality of the evenings in Lerici. The literary history is real, but you don't need to be a poetry reader to appreciate why writers kept coming back â€” the combination of medieval castle, sheltered bay, colorful waterfront buildings, and the clear warm water of the gulf is simply exceptional.

Getting to Lerici from the Cruise Port

Lerici is 10 minutes from La Spezia by taxi, or 30 to 40 minutes by local bus (Line 11) from Piazza Chiodo near the port. The taxi is fast and straightforward â€” the fare is approximately 10 to 12 euros. The bus is significantly cheaper but requires a longer journey time and is less convenient for passengers managing a tight schedule.

There are no direct trains to Lerici â€” the town has no train station, which is part of what has kept it relatively unexplored by independent travelers. Private transfers can also be arranged, which is useful if you want to stop at Fiascherino or Maralunga on the way back. The journey by road offers glimpses of the gulf and the olive-covered hillsides typical of this part of Liguria.

The Castello di Lerici

The medieval castle on the headland above the harbor is the defining landmark of Lerici. Built by the Pisans in the 13th century and subsequently expanded by the Genoese, it sits above the town on a rocky promontory with views that extend across the entire gulf â€” to Porto Venere and the cliff faces of Cinque Terre to the west, and to the beaches of Fiascherino and the outline of Tuscany to the south.

Inside the castle, a museum covers the paleontological history of the region â€” the hills around the Gulf of La Spezia contain significant dinosaur fossil deposits, and the museum has an engaging collection that's more interesting than it sounds. The castle also hosts temporary exhibitions through the year. The courtyard and walls are open to visitors and the views from the ramparts are among the best available from any easily accessible point near La Spezia.

The castle is about a 10-minute walk uphill from the harbor, on well-maintained paths. The climb is gentle enough for most visitors, though those with mobility limitations may prefer to enjoy the views from the waterfront below.

The Waterfront and Piazza Garibaldi

Lerici's harbor is lined with colorful buildings â€” yellows, terracottas, dusty pinks â€” reflected in the calm water of the bay in the mornings. The main piazza, Garibaldi, faces directly onto the harbor and has outdoor restaurants and cafe tables where locals sit through most of the day. It's the kind of piazza that justifies simply sitting and watching the world go by for an hour.

The harbor itself has fishing boats and small pleasure craft moored alongside the sea wall. In summer, small boats can be rented for exploring the coastline â€” the sea caves, the nearby beaches at Fiascherino and Maralunga, and the rocky coves along the headland are all accessible by boat in under 30 minutes. If you have time and interest, hiring a small boat for an hour is an excellent way to see this coast.

Beaches in and Around Lerici

Lerici's main beach is directly in front of the town center â€” a mix of fine gravel and sand with clear water, divided between private beach clubs (stabilimenti) and a free public section. The beach is calm and sheltered, ideal for swimming from June through September. Beach club facilities include sun loungers, parasols, changing rooms, and bar service.

Beyond the main town beach, the coves of Fiascherino and Maralunga, about 15 minutes south by taxi or on foot, offer some of the most beautiful small beaches in the region. These are covered in our guide to the best beaches near La Spezia cruise port.

Eating in Lerici

Seafood is the dominant tradition in Lerici and the quality reflects the proximity to good fishing grounds. Mussels from the Gulf of La Spezia â€” the same Protected Geographical Indication muscoli served across the region â€” appear on every menu. The local anchovies, marinated in lemon juice (alici di Menaica style) or fried with sage, are excellent. Grilled branzino (sea bass) or orata (sea bream) caught that morning is the right main course.

The restaurants around Piazza Garibaldi are good but predictably more expensive than those on the back streets. Walk one or two streets inland and the character changes immediately â€” local trattorie serving daily specials at prices that reflect the fact that they rely on regular customers rather than passing tourists. The house wines are uniformly local Ligurian whites and are worth ordering over anything imported.

How Long Do You Need in Lerici?

Two and a half to three hours in Lerici is enough for a satisfying visit: 30 minutes to walk around the waterfront and piazza, 45 minutes to visit the castle, an hour for lunch, and 15 minutes back to the taxi point. Add another hour if you want to spend time on the beach or take a boat out. The whole excursion from port to port takes about four to five hours.

The Case for Choosing Lerici Over Cinque Terre

Lerici won't appear on anyone's list of Italy's most photogenic destinations. The castle is impressive but not jaw-dropping. The beach is pleasant but not the Caribbean. The food is excellent but similar to what you'd find in any good Ligurian coastal town. What Lerici offers that Cinque Terre increasingly cannot is the feeling of being somewhere that still belongs to Italy rather than to global tourism.

If your ship is arriving on a day when multiple cruise ships are in port and Cinque Terre will be packed, Lerici is the intelligent alternative. If you've seen Cinque Terre before and want something fresh, Lerici rewards you with exactly the Ligurian coastal atmosphere you're looking for without the management overhead.
