What Is Included in the Experience

The truffle hunt excursion from La Spezia is a complete half-day experience that includes:

  • Private transfer from La Spezia cruise port to the forest (approximately 20 to 40 minutes depending on the hunting ground selected for that day)
  • A guided truffle hunt of 60 to 90 minutes with a professional truffle hunter and their trained dog in the Ligurian Apennines
  • Full participation in the hunt â€” you walk with the hunter, watch the dog work, and are present at every find
  • An explanation of truffle varieties, hunting techniques, and the ecology of truffle growth in this region
  • A tasting meal prepared on-site from the day's harvest, featuring fresh truffle in traditional Ligurian preparations
  • Local wine and water with the meal
  • Return transfer to La Spezia cruise port

Total duration: approximately three to four hours from port to port. You will be back at your ship well before any standard all-aboard time.

The Hunt Itself: What Actually Happens

Truffle hunting is a partnership between a trained dog and an experienced hunter, developed over months and years of working the same forests in all conditions. The dog you'll work with on this excursion has been trained specifically for truffle detection from an early age â€” their nose can detect truffle scent through several centimeters of soil even when the human hunter standing directly above it detects nothing.

In the forest, the dog works in widening arcs, nose close to the ground, reading the scent landscape of the forest floor. When they lock onto a truffle signal, their behavior changes â€” the nose goes down hard, the pace slows, the digging begins. The hunter calls the dog back, hands the dog a food reward, and extracts the truffle carefully by hand â€” a practiced movement designed to preserve the underground mycelium network that will produce next year's harvest from the same spot.

You walk through this process with the hunter. You can hold the truffle once it's extracted â€” the weight, the texture, the extraordinary intensity of the scent at close range â€” and you'll understand immediately why they're valued the way they are. If the dog finds multiple truffles, which happens more often than you might expect, you participate in each find.

The Tasting Meal

After the hunt, you'll sit down to eat at a local property near the forest. The meal is prepared fresh from the morning's harvest and follows the Ligurian tradition of letting exceptional ingredients speak for themselves without unnecessary complexity.

A typical tasting includes:

  • Bruschetta of local wood-fired bread with extra virgin olive oil and fresh truffle shaved over the top
  • Fresh egg tagliolini or trofie pasta with truffle â€” the classic preparation of fresh truffle in Italian cooking
  • Seasonal dishes that vary with the time of year and the specific truffles found: soft-boiled egg with truffle in season; risotto with truffle in autumn; local charcuterie with truffle-laced preparations
  • A glass of local Ligurian white wine â€” typically a Vermentino from the Colli di Luni area

The food is exceptional by any standard. When the truffle you eat was in the ground three hours ago and was found by a dog forty minutes into the forest, there is no more direct farm-to-table experience available.

What to Wear and Bring

Forest walking requires clothing suited to outdoor activity. Comfortable closed-toe shoes with some grip â€” trainers or light hiking shoes â€” are essential. The terrain is uneven and there will be undergrowth. Long trousers are recommended in all seasons: protection from scratches in summer and warmth in autumn and winter. A light jacket is useful in cooler months even on sunny days, as the forest temperature is lower than at the coast.

Bring water and any personal medications. A camera or your phone for photographing the dog and the finds â€” these are genuinely photogenic moments. No previous outdoor experience is required and no specialist equipment is needed.
